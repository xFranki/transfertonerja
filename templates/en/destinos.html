🏖️ Nerja – History, Nature, and Sea

Nerja is one of the most attractive destinations on the eastern Costa del Sol. Known for its mild climate year-round, it combines the best of sun and beach tourism with unique cultural and natural spots.

  • Balcón de Europa: The most famous viewpoint, offering spectacular views of the Mediterranean.
  • Nerja Caves: A natural monument with thousands of years of history, concerts, and prehistoric cave paintings.
  • Beaches: Burriana, Calahonda, and El Salón are some of the most visited.
  • Sports and hiking: Routes like the Chíllar River are perfect for warm months.

🏡 Frigiliana – One of Spain’s Most Beautiful Villages

Just a 10-minute drive from Nerja, Frigiliana is a whitewashed mountain gem known for its cobbled streets, white houses, and Andalusian charm.

  • Moorish-Mudéjar historic center.
  • Molasses honey factory: the only active one in Europe.
  • Three Cultures Festival (August): gastronomy, music, and culture.
  • Trails and natural viewpoints ideal for hiking.

🌅 Torrox – The Best Climate in Europe

Located by the sea, Torrox boasts "the best climate in Europe," ideal for tourists all year round. Divided into Torrox Costa (more touristy) and Torrox Pueblo (more traditional).

  • Torrox Costa promenade.
  • Extensive and quiet beaches.
  • Torrox lighthouse and Roman ruins.
  • Markets and typical gastronomy.

🌊 Almuñécar – Costa Tropical and Historical Legacy

Located in the province of Granada, Almuñécar is a key point on the Costa Tropical, where beaches, Roman ruins, and family activities come together.

  • San Miguel Castle.
  • Roman aqueduct and Majuelo Park.
  • Almuñécar Aquarium and Loro Sexi bird park.
  • Beaches like Velilla, San Cristóbal, and La Herradura.
